WHAT TO EXPECT


Trolls call me
moon of dwelling-Rungnir,
giant's wealth-sucker,
storm-sun's bale,
seeress's friendly companion,
guardian of corpse-fjord,
swallower of heaven-wheel;
what is a troll other than that?

We would like to invite you to the third Moose Blues and this year we will discover all about troll mythology and legend.

As the previous years, Moose Blues will be bringing you two journeys to choose from. One journey is through blues, and it will focus on fundamentals and musicality, with a strong focus on fundamentals on Saturday. Our fantastic teachers from Boston will bring their energy all the way from the United States to really help you embrace the power of the blues.

Our other journey will focus on the mythology of the magical country of Norway. Hiding in the forest, trolls are a big part of the traditional stories and culture in this beautiful country. Bring your hiking boots and explore the breathtaking nature with us. The two journeys will reconnect on Sunday, where two parallel set of classes will be held during the day.

There will be classes for those who want to really strengthen their blues basics, and others that will challenge those who have more experience and want to push themselves. Solo classes, connection focus, musicality and quality of movement, and a lot of partying and social dancing of course!

TEACHERS


Jae Wilson

Bristol, UK

I wasn't a dancer the first time I heard live blues in Rochester, New York in 2004. I was just a sophomore in college. I became a dancer and instantly fell in love: with the dance, the people, the stories, the lifestyle. I've been a vagabond, homeless, rich (sorta), flat broke, and busted. But I've seen dance halls in Dublin, Geneva, Heidelberg, Honolulu, New York, Denver, Portland, and a myriad of cities in between. I was blessed to be inspired by Frankie Manning, and I hope to share my love of the dance with you.

Jenny Sowden

NYC, USA

Jenny Sowden is an award winning, full-time, international dance instructor based in New York City. She has been training in dance for over twenty years and teaching for over fifteen years She has studied 34 different styles of movement including Ballet, Tap, Hip Hop, West African, Capoeira, Aikido, Tango, Salsa, Lindy hop, Balboa, and Blues. Her background influences her teaching and general style through musicality, variety, expression and an understanding of body mechanics.

Ane Johansen Heggernes

Norway

Ane started dancing ballet at the age of 5. Since then she has continued to expand her interest for different genres of dance, especially Lindy hop and Blues. As her passion for dance grew she started traveling, teaching and dancing her way through different countries. She is a key member of our local dance scene, teaching and organizing events. In class she focuses on the purity of movement and technical ability together with creativity and joy. She is the latest addition to the Moose Blues team.

PRACTICAL


Travel


There are many airlines flying to Oslo. Your best bet is checking flight searches such as KAYAK for up to date schedules and prices.

Once at Oslo Airport Gardermoen, catch Flytoget to the city center. A slower, but cheaper option, is to take a local train with NSB. If you are flying to Torp or Rygge Airport the airport bus is your best choise.

Getting around in Oslo is easy with the public transport system. Ruter will give you all the information you need.
