WHAT TO EXPECT



Classes

This years edition of Moose Blues will focus on blues aesthetics! There will be 8 hours of classes divided by Friday afternoon and Sunday.

There will be no specialized tracks. Instead you will be able to choose quite freely between the classes. The workshop includes classes on fundamentals, styling, blues aesthetics, and will also have a couple of solo classes.

Parties

There will be parties both Friday and Saturday night, and we guarantee lots of blues dancing and fun. We will also have a tea dance on Sunday. More info coming soon!

Culture

Norway celebrates its constitution day on the 17th of May! The beautiful city of Oslo turns into a huge parade with Norwegians dressing up in traditional costumes.

We will celebrate the day together, taking part in the festivities and having a park BBQ later in the afternoon. All the Scandinavian Royals are coming to town, so you can look forward to a Royal 17th of May Blues Party!

Annette is one of Europe's most prominent proselytisers of blues dance as a teacher, competitor, social dancer, DJ and organiser. Her love of teaching and dancing are infectious and if you've travelled for blues, you probably already knew that!

Carter grew up in the USA but now lives London, where he teaches regularly and is a proud member of Blues Revolution. He's been dancing blues for 10 years and has studied music for 20. This depth of experience informs his teaching, which emphasises musicality, connection and most importantly, fun!

Together, Annette and Carter have been bringing joy and energy to blues dance floors around Europe for years. Sharing a love of blues music and the simple joy of movement, this dynamic duo is passionate about sharing it all with you and helping you to bring out your inner awesome.

Adamo Ciarallo is a dancer, instructor, Dj and event organizer based in London, UK. He teaches regular Blues and Lindy Hop classes, as well as running workshops and performing nationally and internationally. He sees his role as teacher to facilitate the classroom in response to learner’s needs, enabling all students to achieve to their potential. Her background in a variety of solo dances has been invaluable in her partner dancing, and she approaches her blues dancing with grace, fluidity and intense body awareness.

Adamo and Vicci have an incredible chemistry on the dance floor, bringing intensity, elegance and musicality to the dance. Their passion for blues is evident in their inspiring classes, giving dynamic and energetic lessons with a focus on creativity, developing a relationship with the music, and encouraging dancers to find their own blues voice.

17. OF MAY


Norwegian Constitution Day is the National Day of Norway and is an official national holiday observed on May 17 each year. Among Norwegians, the day is referred to simply as syttende mai (meaning May Seventeenth), Nasjonaldagen (The National Day) or Grunnlovsdagen (The Constitution Day), although the latter is less frequent.

A noteworthy aspect of the Norwegian Constitution Day is its very non-military nature. All over Norway, children's parades with an abundance of flags form the central elements of the celebration. Each elementary school district arranges its own parade with marching bands between schools. The parade takes the children through the community, often making stops at homes of senior citizens, war memorials, etc. The longest parade is in Oslo, where some 100,000 people travel to the city centre to participate in the main festivities.

In addition to children's parades, there are parades for the public, where every citizen is welcome to join in. These are led by marching bands and often local boy scouts and girl guides, local choirs, etc. This takes place in the early morning or in the afternoon, before or after the school's parade.

All parades begin or end with speeches. Both grown-ups and older children are invited to speak. After the parades, there are games for the children, and often a lot of icecream, pop, sweets and hotdogs are consumed.


PRACTICAL


Travel


There are many airlines flying to Oslo. Your best bet is checking flight searches such as KAYAK for up to date schedules and prices.

Once at Oslo Airport Gardermoen, catch Flytoget to the city center. A slower, but cheaper option, is to take a local train with NSB. If you are flying to Torp or Rygge Airport the airport bus is your best choise.

Getting around in Oslo is easy with the public transport system. Ruter will give you all the information you need.
